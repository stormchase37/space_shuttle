Space Shuttle Columbia

It took seven years to build Space Shuttle Columbia. Space Shuttle Columbia roared into the sky on April 12, 1981 on STS-1. On July 23, 1999 Columbia deployed the Chandra X-ray Observatory. This observatory has helped answer many fundamental questions about the universe.
On February 1, 2003 Space Shuttle Columbia tragically broke up as it re-entered Earth's atmosphere. The shuttle was damaged during launch when a piece of foam or ice from the external tank came loose and struck the leading edge of Columbia's left wing causing a bowling ball size hole. When Columbia re-entered the atmosphere heat got inside the hole and caused Columbia to break up. This was the second space shuttle lost, Space Shuttle Challenger was lost in 1986.
