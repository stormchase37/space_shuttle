Space Shuttle Atlantis

Space Shuttle Atlantis, NASA's fourth space-rated space shuttle (OV-104) arrived at Kennedy Space Center on April 9, 1985. Construction on Atlantis began on March 3, 1980 and took four years to complete. During Atlantis' early years, it carried several Department of Defense payloads to orbit which still remain classified today.
Atlantis' last flight was in July 2011, after Atlantis landed NASA began preparing the space shuttle for public display at Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex in Florida. On November 2, 2012 was the final goodbye to Atlantis as the space shuttle entered retirement for future generations to learn about the space shuttle program. Atlantis spent 307 days in space and traveled 125,935,769 miles. Completed 200 orbits on 33 missions.
