Space Shuttle Discovery

It took four years to build Space Shuttle Discovery and was completed in October 1983. Space Shuttle Discovery (OV-103) launched on its maiden vogage in August 30, 1984 on flight 41-D. Before launching on its maiden vogage it arrived at the Kennedy Space Center in Novemeber 1983 to undergo extensive checks and testing of all systems. Space Shuttle Discovery was the workhorse of the shuttle fleet. After the loss of Space Shuttle Columbia, Discovery answered the call once again for the Return to Flight mission. During Space Shuttle Discovery's lifetime, it flew 365 days in space. That is more than any other space shuttle in the fleet. Discovery flew a total of 148,221,675 miles in 39 space missions. Discovery's last flight was STS-133 launching on February 24, 2011 and landing on March 9, 2011.

In the history of Space Shuttle Discovery, one of its big achievements was deploying the Hubble Space Telescope on April 24, 1990. The Hubble Space Telescope is still in operation today 24 years later.
